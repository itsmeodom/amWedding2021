About

Melissa Tran & Adam Araza

A Tea Ceremony is a traditional Vietnamese gathering celebrating the union of a bride and groom as well as their respective families. On the day of Melissa and Adam's ceremony, Adam's guests will meet at his home and caravan together to the home of Melissa's parents. Upon arrival, they will be greeted and welcomed into the home by a member of Melissa's family. Before entering, Adam's family provide 8 gifts to be exchanged with Melissa's family. These gifts symbolize the prosperity and good fortune a groom can bring to a bride's family. Once inside, Adam and Melissa will serve tea for their parents and their elder relatives. This is gesture serves as a symbolic thank you to their families for all they have done, as well as a way to ask for a blessing of their union. After serving tea, we will move to the backyard where a lunch will be hosted. The ceremony will pay respect to Melissa's Vietnamese heritage as well as honoring both Melissa and Adam's parents, families and ancestors. It will be considered Melissa and Adam's cultural wedding day.

The Gifts

  • Teapot
  • Betel leaf with areca nut - traditional symbols of love and marriage
  • Cash / Jewelry - the dowry
  • Tea
  • Fruits
  • Red Wine
  • Sweetheart Cakes
  • Pork Belly

Other Information

Common Questions and Things to Know

So what am I supposed to do as a guest?

Meet at Adam's house at 10:00. We'll organize a carpool and caravan to Melissa's parents house. When we arrive at Melissa's parents house, please line up behind my parents and the 8 people carrying the gifts. We'll be welcomed into the home. Tea will be served inside, and then we'll move to the backyard for lunch.


Gifts

We are not expecting gifts, and it is completely appropriate to to not bring a gift. If you would like to give a gift, we are registered at Crate and Barrel and Amazon. We also are also accepting donations toward our travel fund and our home savings fund.


Vaccinations

For the safety of our families and friends, we ask that all guests be fully vaccinated for Covid-19 before attending our Tea Ceremony. If you are unable to receive the vaccination, we politely ask that you decline our invite.


What Should I Wear?

This is a semi-formal event. Jackets and slacks or dresses. Some members of Melissa and Adam's families will be wearing traditional Vietnamese or Filipino formal attire. It's still the bay area in November, so it might be cold, but it also could be hot. The lunch will be outdoor, so please be comfortable.


Can I Bring a Guest or +1?

To keep the celebration intimate, we are keeping the guest list limited. Please do not bring someone who was not explicitly invited without first speaking with Melissa or Adam.


Who will be receiving tea?

Keeping with tradition, during the ceremony, tea will be poured to relatives who are older than Melissa or Adam - ie their 'elders'. Others can receive tea, but will not be served tea during the ceremony.


Is this our Wedding Day?

The tea ceremony is traditionally considered a wedding day in Vietnamese culture. We will celebrate both November 6 and December 4 as our special days, but we will not legally be married until December.
